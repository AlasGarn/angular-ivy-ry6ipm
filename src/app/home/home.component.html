ABOUT ME

I specialize in studying complex biomolecular mechanisms through computer simulations. My aptitude lies with physical chemistry, particularly, biophysical models based on methods ranging from quantum mechanics to coarse-grained, with a focus on Markov state models and enhanced sampling. Overall, my work has contributed to our understanding of biophysics of multiple protein systems, specifically, transcription with RNA polymerase II and antibody design. Currently I am a post-doctoral researcher with Xuhui Huang at UW Madison

LATEST WORK

Mutations in PP2A hack its allosteric network

Mutations in the protein phosphatase 2A, a major cell cycle regulator, cause cancer and intellectual disability. I found that mutations in this protein rewire a communication network underlying its dynamics to exert their harmful effect. Learn more...

It takes more than one hydrogen bond to stop a transcribing RNA polymerase II

The RNA polymerase II transcribes almost all protein coding genes in eukaryotes and is very sensitive to modifications of the DNA. By using Markov state models, I found that 5-carboxylcytosine - an epigenetic modification of cytosine - can stop the Pol II by interfering with its internal machinery in suprizingly many ways.
